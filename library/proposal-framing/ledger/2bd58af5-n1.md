1. **Decidable-out-of-flow, across a resume boundary** — a refinement of the out-of-flow failure
   mode. The proposer surfaced a Y/N decision whose decidable content (a checklist the partner had to
   act on) lived in an *earlier message of the same conversation*, then asked the partner to act
   after a context-switch/resume. On resume the referenced content was scrolled out of view, so at
   the moment of the ask the partner had nothing to act on. Fix: re-render the decidable core in-flow
   *in the same surface that asks for the dispose* — "earlier in this conversation" becomes
   out-of-flow once a resume/intermission boundary intervenes. In-flow is relative to the partner's
   *current view*, not the conversation's full history.

**Pattern:** the same single drift the origin ledger names — cost silently migrating from proposer
back to disposer — reproduced independently in a second dyad's practice (different operator, different
domain), confirming breadth and adding one boundary refinement.
