## Claim (the falsifiable boundaries)

The Dyad Practice navigates **two hard boundaries** — naming where it fails is part of holding it
honestly, not a disclaimer:

1. **The Hallucination Edge.** The AI will confidently hallucinate. Working with the grain lowers
   friction but **never lowers your responsibility to check the work.** The practice **fails the moment
   you accept the Agent's output without stress-testing it** — wu-wei without falsification.
2. **An Incomplete Library.** The set of playbooks is **explicitly unfinished.** Discovering and proving
   new routines is the **active frontier** — the practice is a living, falsifiable inquiry, not a
   finished method.

## Refutation (how this boundary-claim dies)

- A Dyad reliably produces the `+1` **without** stress-testing (pure wu-wei) → the Hallucination-Edge
  boundary is mis-drawn.
- The library ever **closes** — a complete, finished set with no frontier → the Incomplete-Library
  boundary dissolves; the practice would no longer be a living inquiry.
