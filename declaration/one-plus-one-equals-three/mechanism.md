## Claim (the falsifiable +1)

The goal of Human–AI collaboration isn't agreement; it is **synergy: `1 + 1 = 3`.** A Human and an
Agent operating as an irreducible team produce emergent work neither could reach alone — *more* than the
sum, not the averaged-down compromise (`1 + 1 = 2`) of a tool answering a prompt.

The synergy is earned through **two principles**:

1. **Work with the grain (Wu-wei).** Don't force the AI against its nature; treat it as a reasoning
   engine, not a database. Lowering friction raises the ceiling on what the pair can reach.
2. **Stress-test everything (falsifiability).** You don't earn the `+1` through blind agreement. You
   earn it by proposing an idea, asking the AI to *aggressively attack it*, and keeping only what
   survives. This is the anti-sycophancy guarantee: without it, synergy inverts to `1 + 1 = 1` (mutual
   confirmation).

## Refutation (how the tenet would die)

- **Mutual-confirmation drift** — pairs that "agree" produce no surviving contest → the output is `1+1=1`
  (or `2`), not `3`. If the practice consistently yields no more than a good solo prompt, the `+1` is a
  fiction.
- **Wu-wei without the stress-test** — friction lowered but nothing falsified → comfortable, unearned
  agreement.
- **Stress-test without wu-wei** — contest forced against the grain → friction so high the pair can't
  function. Either principle alone fails; the `+1` needs both.
